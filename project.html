MUSEUM OF CANDY

Chocolate is a preparation of roasted and ground cacao seeds that is made in the form of a liquid, paste, or in a block. The earliest signs of use are associated with Olmec sites (within what would become Mexico’s post-colonial territory) suggesting consumption of chocolate beverages, dating from the 19th century BC.
